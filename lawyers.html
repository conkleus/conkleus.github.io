After graduating from the U.S. Naval Academy, Bill Conkle served in the Navy in Europe and Vietnam as a shipboard and intelligence officer. He attended the UCLA School of Law, where he focused on business, trade regulation and tax matters, and was on the Moot Court. Bill authored an article on mechanic's liens for the UCLA Alaska Law Review. He was selected as a John Olin Mathison Fellow in Law and Economics by the UCLA Department of Economics and UCLA School of Law, and was a member of this innovative program that focused on the economics of business firms and legal regulation.

AREAS OF PRACTICE

  • Brand Protection for consumer products companies, including creating and protecting intellectual property and pursuing judicial and administrative remedies against infringers
  • Trial practice, including jury, non-jury and arbitration proceedings. Bill has obtained several multi-million verdicts that were affirmed and/or paid
  • Insurance law for policy owners and persons protected by insurance to enforce their rights against insurance companies
  • Drafting agreements concerning real property and handling jury trials concerning real estate issues
  • Business law, including the organization and operation of business entities and the drafting and enforcement of contacts
